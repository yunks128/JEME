# SWOT - Surface Water and Ocean Topography

## Overview

SWOT (Surface Water and Ocean Topography) is a joint NASA/CNES satellite mission that uses Ka-band radar interferometry (KaRIn) to make the first global survey of Earth's surface water with unprecedented spatial resolution. Launched in December 2022, SWOT measures ocean surface topography and inland water body elevation, providing two-dimensional maps of water height that were previously impossible from space.

## Background

SWOT addresses a critical gap in our ability to observe water on Earth's surface. Traditional nadir altimeters measure along a single ground track, but SWOT's wide-swath interferometric radar captures a 120 km-wide swath, enabling measurement of lakes, reservoirs, rivers, and fine-scale ocean features. The mission is a collaboration between NASA (managed at JPL) and CNES (French Space Agency), with contributions from the Canadian and UK space agencies.

## Key Features

- **Wide-Swath Altimetry**: KaRIn instrument measures a 120 km swath (two 50 km swaths separated by a 20 km nadir gap)
- **High Spatial Resolution**: ~100 m pixel resolution for water surface elevation
- **Dual Frequency**: Ka-band interferometry combined with nadir altimeter
- **Inland Water**: First satellite to observe rivers >100 m wide and lakes >250 m × 250 m globally
- **Submesoscale Oceanography**: Resolves ocean features at 15-25 km scales
- **21-Day Repeat**: Complete global coverage with 21-day exact repeat orbit

## Scientific Applications

- **River Discharge**: Estimating river flow rates from water surface elevation and slope
- **Lake & Reservoir Storage**: Monitoring water storage changes in inland water bodies
- **Submesoscale Ocean Dynamics**: Observing eddies, fronts, and filaments at fine scales
- **Coastal Processes**: Sea level variability and tidal dynamics in coastal zones
- **Flood Mapping**: Monitoring inundation extent and water levels during flood events
- **Water Resources Management**: Supporting freshwater availability assessments globally

## Technical Details

**Mission Type**: Wide-Swath Radar Altimetry
**Domain**: Global (oceans and inland water)
**Spatial Resolution**: ~100 m (pixel), 2 km (ocean height accuracy)
**Temporal Resolution**: 21-day repeat cycle
**Orbit**: 891 km altitude, 77.6° inclination
**Agencies**: NASA (JPL), CNES, CSA, UKSA
**Key Instrument**: Ka-band Radar Interferometer (KaRIn)
**Launch Date**: December 16, 2022
