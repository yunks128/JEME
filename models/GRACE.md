# GRACE - Gravity Recovery and Climate Experiment

## Overview

GRACE (Gravity Recovery and Climate Experiment) and its successor GRACE-FO (Follow-On) are joint NASA/DLR satellite missions that track changes in Earth's gravity field with unprecedented precision. By measuring tiny variations in the distance between twin satellites, GRACE maps monthly changes in mass distribution across the planet — revealing how water moves through the Earth system.

## Background

Launched in 2002, GRACE revolutionized our ability to monitor large-scale mass changes on Earth. The mission concept, based on satellite-to-satellite tracking, enables detection of gravity field variations caused by redistribution of water, ice, and solid Earth mass. GRACE-FO, launched in 2018, continues these measurements with improved laser ranging interferometry, ensuring continuity of this critical climate data record managed at JPL.

## Key Features

- **Twin Satellite Design**: Two co-orbiting satellites ~220 km apart measuring inter-satellite distance changes
- **Monthly Gravity Fields**: Global gravity solutions at ~300 km spatial resolution
- **Mass Change Detection**: Sensitivity to mass changes equivalent to ~1 cm of water over 300 km
- **Multi-Decade Record**: Continuous measurements from 2002-2017 (GRACE) and 2018-present (GRACE-FO)
- **Mascon Solutions**: JPL mascon products providing gridded mass change estimates
- **Laser Ranging Interferometry**: GRACE-FO includes experimental laser ranging for improved precision

## Scientific Applications

- **Terrestrial Water Storage**: Monitoring groundwater depletion, drought, and flood conditions
- **Ice Sheet Mass Balance**: Quantifying ice loss from Greenland and Antarctica
- **Sea Level Change**: Partitioning sea level rise into ice melt, thermal expansion, and water storage components
- **Glacial Isostatic Adjustment**: Constraining post-glacial rebound models
- **Ocean Bottom Pressure**: Measuring deep ocean circulation changes
- **Earthquake & Tectonics**: Detecting large-scale mass redistribution from major earthquakes

## Technical Details

**Mission Type**: Satellite Gravimetry
**Domain**: Global
**Spatial Resolution**: ~300 km (spherical harmonic degree ~60)
**Temporal Resolution**: Monthly solutions
**Orbit**: ~500 km altitude, near-polar (89° inclination)
**Agencies**: NASA (JPL), DLR (German Aerospace Center)
**Data Products**: Level-1B (ranging), Level-2 (spherical harmonics), Level-3 (mascons)
